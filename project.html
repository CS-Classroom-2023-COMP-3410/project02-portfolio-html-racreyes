Internship Experience

  • Blizzard Entertainment

    During my time at Blizzard, I felt I learned the most I've ever learned in my life. I learned not only so much about coding but also living alone in a new state, adapting to new driving habits, trying new foods, meeting new people, and so much more. I am genuinely so grateful to have had this experience and on top of that just learn so much more about softare engineering and learning from senior developers.
    Focusing on the technical side of things, I was tasked with moving our current system of sending builds out internally for testing --into a more efficient and straightforward method. I accomplished this with the use of automating calls with the use of Jenkins as well as Google Cloud Functions and AWSS3 buckets. I learned a great deal about GCP as well as Jenkins and Groovy which Jenkins uses.

  • Lockheed Martin Space

    During my time at Lockheed Martin, it was definitely a wild ride. I got the internship right out of high school barely knowing any kind of coding or having any experience with computers. For the first 2 years, I definitely was one of the less experienced interns there with many having a couple years of coding experience already. It was such a fun experience and I got to present to members of the government and high leveled VPs. I also made a couple friends and made really good memories with the other interns.

    Year 1:
    Designed and developed the user interface for an iPad application that incorporated Augmented Reality (AR) and supported concepts of Generative Design. Gained foundational experience in UI/UX development and AR integration while collaborating with a multidisciplinary team.
    Year 2:
    Collaborated with a full-time team to implement new features for an in-house application designed to assist in creating presentations and demonstrations for a proprietary three-screen device. Leveraged Unity to develop and enhance functionalities, ensuring seamless user experiences during client-facing presentations.
    Year 3:
    Contributed to an end-to-end proof-of-concept project aimed at firefighting solutions. This included gathering and displaying real-time weather data and location information on both a website and an AR-enabled HoloLens headset. Designed the solution to aid firefighters by providing critical, actionable data in a user-friendly interface. Delivered the concept as part of a broader innovation initiative to present advanced firefighting technologies.
